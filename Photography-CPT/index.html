Photography CPT

Coded and Created by Mateo T

Topic

My topic is Light Painting/Photography. I took photos in the dark or darker environments with my flashlight to show light movement with long shutter speeds. I took photos in dark areas and used different light sources and movement patterns to create a figure using that light (like christmas light, phone flashlight or plastic bag filled with light). I chose this because I think that this is something unique and difficult to produce, for a challenge.

Reference

My first reference is Kyle Nutt. www.instagram.com/kylenutt

His genre is mostly photography, and playing around with light concepts. This photo’s camera angle is eye level/worm’s eye. I think I will play around with the worm's eye view, but most of my photos will be eye level. This scene is hard lit on the portal, and all the light comes from that portal. This photo demonstrates a wide angle view and motion blur. This photo will be my main struggle to try to recreate, at least the main idea should be done. It gives me a visual of what I can do with light and the layering off multiple photos. I hope to copy this type of shot in 1 of my photos.

  • Wide angle
  • Bright light from subject
  • Light painting
  • High contrast
  • Photo cropping and layering

My second, and more inspired reference is Patrick Rochon. www.patrickthelightpainter.com

Patrick Rochon’s genre is all about light painting and the beautiful way of conducting it. This photo emphazises motion with light. It practically embodies what I had in mind for this CPT. It is eye level, wide angle and motion blurred. I know what I make won’t be as cool, but something similar would be great.

  • Wide angle
  • Bright light from subject

Final Thoughts

Did this project meet your expectations?

This project was something that I knew at the start that I would have to be creative about. The final results surpassed my expectations because what I had planned on the proposal was only half, or a third of what I actually ended up doing. I had originally planned to just draw with my phone flashlight, and create a portal, but over time I just found better ways of using light painting. I also got a lot of help with photographing and with subjects (thanks for that).


What is the most succesful part of this project?

The most successful part of my project would have to be the color depth in my photos. Because I used a lot of contrast in my photos, it brings out the color, which makes the photos pop. So the editing part is the most successful part of this process. The least succesful part would probably be the proposal because it did not match what I ended up doing (like with the car).


What would you change if you had to redo this project?

If I were to redo this project, I would have started with higher expectations and vision for what photos I was to shoot. I would also take more time to plan out photoshoot and plan. That means that my proposal would be more accurate and I would have shot my photos in quicker time because I would know exactly what to shoot and how, instead of figuring it out midway. An example of this could be when I was trying to shoot my tennis photo, and I had to no idea how to do it and it took a while to just to even start (because I never proposed it nor planned it fully).


What are 2 skills you've improved on during this course?

The two skills i've improved on since the beginning of this course would have to be my photoshop skills and my ability to use manual mode. The more experience I had with photoshop the more I figured out the tools, like the adjustments tab or the spot healing brush tool. My skills using manual mode mostly improved during the end of the course while playing around with the ISO for the exposure assignement, or the shutter speed for this CPT. For photography, I found that practice made improvement.
